Methylene Blue (Methylthioninium Chloride) for Vasoplegia

Critical Care Clinical Guideline - UNLICENSED & NON-FORMULARY
Version 1.00
Author: Matt Govier (ICU Consultant)
Valid: May 2023 - May 2027
Setting: A600, C604, Weston ICU
⚠️ CONSULTANT-INITIATED THERAPY ONLY Methylene blue is unlicensed for vasoplegia and non-formulary. This therapy should only be initiated following consultant approval. Non-formulary paperwork is required but should not delay treatment. Contact ICU Pharmacist for assistance.

Setting

Adult ICU units at UHBW: General ICU (A600), Cardiac ICU (C604), and Weston ICU

For Staff

ICU nurses, advanced critical care practitioners, and medical staff

Patient Group

Patients with severe vasoplegic shock (post-CPB, sepsis, toxicology) refractory to standard vasopressor therapy

Indication & Mechanism of Action

Mechanism: Methylene blue competitively inhibits nitric oxide by binding to the iron haem-moiety of soluble guanylate cyclase enzyme. This blocks cGMP-mediated calcium extrusion from vascular smooth muscle, counteracting vasodilation and reversing vasoplegia.

Clinical Context

Patients with severe vasoplegic shock present significant treatment challenges. Excessive catecholamine doses cause serious adverse effects including arrhythmias, myocardial ischaemia, and increased mortality. Methylene blue acts on non-catecholamine pathways, potentially reducing vasopressor requirements and limiting catecholamine-related complications.

When to Consider Methylene Blue

All of the following criteria must be met:

  • Adequate fluid resuscitation - Patient must be appropriately volume loaded
  • High-dose noradrenaline - Dose >0.5 mcg/kg/min
  • Vasopressin therapy - Dose >0.02 units/min
  • Vasopressor-dose corticosteroids - Hydrocortisone 50mg QDS IV
  • Optimised cardiac output - Inodilator agents reduced whilst maintaining adequate cardiac output
  • Confirmed vasoplegic state - Documented by cardiac output monitoring or echocardiography
  • Progressive deterioration - Despite maximal conventional therapy
Essential Prerequisite Cardiac output monitoring or echocardiographic assessment must confirm a vasoplegic state (low SVR with preserved or elevated cardiac output) prior to administration.

Common Aetiologies of Vasoplegia

  • Post-cardiopulmonary bypass (CPB) - particularly prolonged bypass times
  • Septic shock - especially gram-negative sepsis
  • Toxicology - calcium channel blocker overdose, beta-blocker toxicity
  • Anaphylaxis
  • Post-cardiac arrest

Prescribing & Administration

⚠️ CRITICAL COMPATIBILITY INFORMATION Methylene blue is INCOMPATIBLE with sodium chloride 0.9%. All dilutions and line flushing MUST use glucose 5% only. Failure to follow this can result in precipitation and line occlusion.
Important Dosing Considerations
  • All doses calculated using LEAN body weight
  • Central venous administration recommended
  • Peripheral administration only in emergencies - maximum concentration 1mg/ml
  • Available as 5mg/ml and 10mg/ml preparations - exercise caution

Initial Bolus Dose

Dose: 1-2 mg/kg (lean body weight)
Preparation: Dilute to 50ml total volume with glucose 5%
Administration: Infuse over 15-60 minutes via central line

Renal Impairment Adjustment

Consider reducing initial bolus to <1mg/kg in patients with renal impairment

Ongoing Therapy (if positive response to initial bolus)

Choose either repeated boluses OR continuous infusion:

Monitoring & Adverse Effects

Essential Monitoring Requirements
  • Continuous SpO2 monitoring during and for 4 hours post-administration
  • CNS assessment for 4 hours after each dose
  • Methaemoglobin levels every 4 hours if using infusion or repeated boluses
  • Continuous cardiac monitoring
  • Blood pressure monitoring
  • Arterial blood gas analysis as required

Adverse Effects and Management

Adverse Effect Monitoring & Management
CNS Toxicity
Dizziness, headache, tremors, mental confusion		 Monitoring: Neurological observations during and for 4 hours post-dose
Management: Minimise dose, provide supportive care. If features of CNS toxicity develop, monitor closely and give supportive treatment.
Pulse Oximetry Interference
Falsely low SpO2 readings		 Monitoring: Continuous SpO2 during and for 4 hours post-administration
Management: If SpO2 appears falsely low, check arterial PaO2 on blood gas. Provide supplemental oxygen as clinically indicated.
Cardiac Arrhythmias Monitoring: Continuous ECG monitoring
Management: Consider reducing methylene blue or other vasoactive agents. Treat arrhythmias with standard antiarrhythmic protocols.
Hypertension Monitoring: Continuous arterial pressure monitoring
Management: Assess fluid status. Consider reducing methylene blue or other vasopressor doses.
Hypotension Monitoring: Continuous arterial pressure monitoring
Management: Assess fluid status. Consider increasing other vasopressor agents.
Methaemoglobinaemia Monitoring: Check MetHb levels on arterial blood gas every 4 hours during infusion/repeated boluses
Management: If MetHb >5%, reduce or stop infusion and do not give further boluses.
Gastrointestinal
Nausea		 Management: Consider antiemetic therapy
Blue Discolouration
Urine, stools, saliva		 Management: Reassure patient and healthcare staff. This is expected and harmless.
Positive Response Indicators: Reduction in vasopressor requirements, improved blood pressure, increased systemic vascular resistance
Duration of Effect: If effective, expect response within 1-2 hours of initial bolus. Duration of action typically 2-4 hours, hence timing of repeat dosing.

Safety Considerations & Drug Interactions

⚠️ MHRA SAFETY WARNING - Serotonergic Drug Interactions The MHRA has issued specific guidance on methylene blue use with serotonergic antidepressants due to risk of central nervous system toxicity.

Serotonergic Drug Interactions

High-Risk Interactions

Intravenous methylthioninium chloride should be AVOIDED in patients recently treated with:

  • Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs)
  • Clomipramine
  • Venlafaxine
  • Other serotonergic antidepressants

If Use Cannot Be Avoided:

  • Use the lowest possible dose
  • Observe patient closely for CNS effects for up to 4 hours after administration
  • Watch for signs of serotonin syndrome: agitation, confusion, neuromuscular hyperactivity, autonomic instability
  • If CNS toxicity develops, monitor closely and provide supportive care

Contraindications

Absolute Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to methylene blue
  • Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency - risk of severe haemolysis
  • Recent use of serotonergic antidepressants (relative - see above)

Special Populations

Renal Impairment

  • Reduce initial bolus to <1mg/kg
  • Use lower end of infusion range (0.25 mg/kg/hr)
  • Monitor closely for adverse effects

Hepatic Impairment

  • No experience in severe hepatic impairment
  • Use with extreme caution
  • Consider dose reduction

Compatibility

Compatible with: Glucose 5% only
Incompatible with: Sodium chloride 0.9% and all saline-containing solutions
