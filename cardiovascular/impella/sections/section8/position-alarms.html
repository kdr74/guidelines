Good Impella position is key in preventing complications and maximizing device function. If there is an incorrect position as described below, this may be accompanied by haemolysis.

Incorrect positions are often picked up automatically by the AIC, utilising the placement and motor current waveforms. Even if an alarm has not been activated ‚Äì reviewing the waveforms can tell you if the catheter position may be incorrect. It is important to review the waveforms after each patient position change.

üî¥ Impella Position in Ventricle (RED ALARM)

Figure 10: Waveforms seen in ventricular placement of the Impella Catheter

Waveform Pattern:

  • Inflow, outflow and open pressure lumen are all in LV
  • The aortic and LV placement signals will be almost identical with a LOW diastolic pressure (both will look like an LV trace)
  • The motor current waveform is flattened and less pulsatile

Management:

  1. Contact Cardiology SpR on call for catheter re-positioning
  2. Minor adjustments can be performed on ICU. Before re-positioning P level needs to be reduced to P-2. Repositioning can be done with:
    • Echo guidance (See Appendix 3)
    • The AIC Repositioning guide ‚Äì Select "MENU" softkey and then select "Repositioning guide" and follow the instructions on the screen
  3. If unsuccessful the patient should be brought back to the cath lab for re-positioning under fluoroscopic guidance
  4. The interventional cardiology consultant on call must be informed any time an Impella needs to be repositioned

üî¥ Impella Position in Aorta (RED ALARM)

Figure 11: Waveforms seen in aortic placement of the Impella Catheter

Waveform Pattern:

  • Inflow, outflow and open pressure lumen are all in aorta
  • The aortic and LV placement signals will be almost identical with a HIGH diastolic pressure (both will look like an arterial line trace)
  • The motor current waveform is flattened and less pulsatile
  • The position of the pigtail could either be still in the ventricle, or also in the aorta

Management:

  1. Reduce the P level to P-2
  2. Contact Cardiology SpR on call for echocardiogram to determine catheter position
  3. If the pigtail is:
    • IN THE AORTA ‚Äì the patient will need to return to the cath labs for re-insertion of the Impella catheter into the ventricle over a stiff wire
    • IN THE VENTRICLE ‚Äì Impella repositioning can be performed under echocardiographic guidance on the ICU in the first instance (see Appendix 3). If this is unsuccessful the patient should return to the cath labs for fluoroscopic guided repositioning
  4. The interventional cardiology consultant on call must be informed any time an Impella needs to be repositioned

üî¥ Impella Position Unknown (RED ALARM)

Cause: Low native heart pulsatility

Waveform Pattern:

  • The placement waveform signals (either aortic or LV) pulse pressure is narrowed to <20mmHg
  • The motor current remains pulsatile but dampened

Management: Reassess patient's LV contractility and Impella position with echocardiography

üî¥ Placement Signal Low (RED ALARM)

Cause: If the inflow and outflow areas are in the LV (i.e. the Impella device is too deep in the LV), but the open pressure area remains in the aortic root close to the aortic valve

Waveform Pattern:

  • Correct aortic and LV placement signals
  • A dampened and less pulsatile motor current waveform

Management: Echocardiographic confirmation of the Impella position ‚Äì with manipulation as per "Impella position in ventricle" alarm
