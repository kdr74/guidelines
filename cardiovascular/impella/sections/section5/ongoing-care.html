Ongoing Care Requirements

Patients with an Impella device in situ require intensive monitoring and specific care protocols to ensure optimal device function and patient safety.

General Patient Care

ü©∫ Anticoagulation

Unfractionated heparin infusion, as per ECMO guidelines

  • Target Anti-Xa: 0.3-0.5
  • Anti-Xa levels should be sent at a minimum of every 6 hours
  • Do not give heparin bolus
  • Follow ICU heparin protocol for dose adjustments

üîç Access Site Management

  • Regular assessment of access site for bleeding/haematoma
  • Monitor sheath position
  • Check for signs of infection
  • Document any changes in appearance

ü¶µ Knee Immobiliser

The leg with the Impella in situ must stay straight at the knee

  • Apply knee immobiliser where required
  • Patient may be flexed at the hip to a maximum of 30 degrees
  • Do not raise head of bed higher than 30-degree angle

Specific Nursing Management

üìä Hourly Monitoring and Documentation

Monitor and document the following parameters every hour:

  • Impella flow rates from AIC screen
  • Purge pressure
  • Purge fluid rate
  • Placement signal

Documentation: Add the CIS "Impella" row group on Phillips ICCA

Hourly Limb Assessment

  • Monitor pedal pulses manually and with Doppler
  • Assess colour, temperature, and sensation of limb
  • Check capillary refill time
  • Document any changes immediately

Hourly Access Site Check

  • Assess for haematoma formation
  • Check for bleeding or oozing
  • Monitor dressing integrity

4-Hourly Monitoring

  • Impella position on catheter shaft - document cm marking at sheath hub

Position Verification After Patient Repositioning

Impella position should be checked using the AIC waveform screen after each repositioning of the patient

  • Review placement waveforms
  • Check motor current pattern
  • Contact cardiology SpR if position alarms triggered

Safety Precautions

‚ö†Ô∏è Head of Bed Position

Do not raise the head of the bed to higher than a 30-degree angle

‚ö†Ô∏è Purge Tubing Management

To prevent purge tubing from kinking:

  • Do not allow the red Impella plug to hang freely from the catheter
  • Do not bend the catheter near the red Impella plug
  • Ensure tubing is properly secured and supported

Shift Safety Checks

At the start of each shift, verify:

  • Impella catheter position (cm marking at sheath hub)
  • All connections secure (purge cassette, white cable, luer locks)
  • Purge system functioning (moving bar on AIC screen)
  • No alarms active or silenced
  • Heparin infusion running at correct rate
  • Knee immobiliser in place and secure
  • Access site dressing intact
  • Limb perfusion adequate
  • Spare purge cassette available at bedside

Emergency Situations

üö® Cardiac Arrest Management

In the event of cardiac arrest:

  • Impella support should continue
  • Reduce flow rates down to P2
  • Defibrillation is safe during Impella support
  • Following ROSC, check Impella position with echo
  • If adequate position confirmed, return P-level to previous support level

Multidisciplinary Care

Daily review required from:

  • ICU consultant
  • Cardiology consultant (CCU or interventional)
  • Bedside nurse
  • Physiotherapist (passive range of motion exercises for unaffected limbs)

Regular input from:

  • Cardiology SpR (on-call 24/7, Ext #6527)
  • Cardiac physiologist (for AIC troubleshooting)
  • Pharmacy (anticoagulation management)
  • Dietitian (nutritional support)
