Catheter Preparation

Proper catheter preparation is essential for safe and effective Impella deployment. The preparation process must be performed using strict sterile technique.

Purge Solution Preparation

Two purge solution options are available. Option A is preferred:

Option A (Preferred): Dextrose 5% with Sodium Bicarbonate

500ml dextrose 5% with 12.5ml of 8.4% sodium bicarbonate injected into it

Option B (Alternative): Dextrose 5% with Heparin

500ml dextrose 5% with 25 IU of heparin per ml (12,500 IU of heparin in 500ml bag)

Catheter Setup Procedure

  1. Open sterile field

    Using sterile technique, open Impella kit onto sterile field

  2. Hand off purge cassette

    Scrubbed assistant hands the purge cassette and spike to the physiologist, retaining the luer end

  3. Remove Y-connector if present

    If there is a Y-connector on the luer end of the purge kit, remove it

  4. Spike purge solution

    Physiologist spikes the purge solution bag and inserts purge cassette into AIC, as indicated on screen

  5. Start auto setup

    Auto setup will start. Follow on-screen commands

  6. Insert white cable

    Insert the white cable into the AIC when instructed by the on-screen prompts

  7. Connect purge cassette to catheter

    When prompted, sterile person connects luer of purge cassette to purge sidearm of the Impella catheter

  8. Prime the pump

    Scrubbed assistant squeezes white clamp when indicated on AIC, to prime the pump

Key Points

  • Maintain strict sterile technique throughout the preparation process
  • Follow AIC on-screen prompts carefully - the system will guide you through each step
  • Ensure complete priming - all air must be removed from the purge system before insertion
  • Check connections - verify all luer locks are secure before proceeding
  • Confirm purge flow - verify purge system is functioning before catheter insertion

Roles and Responsibilities

Cardiac Physiologist:

  • Operates the AIC console
  • Spikes purge solution bag
  • Inserts purge cassette into AIC
  • Follows on-screen setup commands
  • Monitors priming process

Scrubbed Assistant (Sterile):

  • Opens sterile kit
  • Hands off purge cassette to physiologist
  • Connects purge cassette to catheter when prompted
  • Operates white clamp for priming
  • Maintains sterile field

Operator (Interventional Cardiologist/Cardiac Surgeon):

  • Oversees entire preparation process
  • Ensures sterile technique maintained
  • Verifies system ready before insertion

Troubleshooting During Preparation

If air bubbles persist after priming:

  • Continue squeezing white clamp until all air removed
  • Check all connections for leaks
  • Ensure purge bag is properly spiked

If AIC displays error during setup:

  • Follow on-screen troubleshooting prompts
  • Verify white cable is properly connected
  • Check purge cassette is correctly inserted
  • Contact Abiomed support if persistent errors (001-800-422-8666)
