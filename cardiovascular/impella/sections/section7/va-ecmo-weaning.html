Section 7: Weaning During VA-ECMO

Version 3.2 | March 2025 ‚Äì December 2026

Management of patients on both VA-ECMO and Impella requires coordinated multidisciplinary decision-making.

This is one of the most complex scenarios in cardiac critical care, requiring careful balancing of support from both devices.

Decision-Making Team

üë• Multidisciplinary Consultation Required

The following consultants must collaborate on weaning decisions:

  • ECMO consultant
  • Interventional cardiology consultant
  • Imaging cardiology consultant

Assessment Parameters

Weaning decisions are based on comprehensive assessment of multiple parameters:

Key Assessment Criteria

  • Native cardiac contractility and output
    • Serial echocardiography to assess LV function
    • Pulse contour analysis when available
    • Assessment during temporary flow reductions
  • LV size and dimensions
    • Echo measurements of LV end-diastolic diameter
    • Assessment of chamber dilation
    • Wall motion abnormalities
  • Pulmonary congestion
    • Chest X-ray findings
    • Lung ultrasound B-lines
    • PCWP measurements if PAC in situ
  • Overall haemodynamics
    • Blood pressure and MAP
    • End-organ perfusion markers
    • Lactate and ScvO‚ÇÇ
    • Urine output

Weaning Strategy

There is no single protocol for weaning ECMO and Impella simultaneously. The strategy must be individualized based on:

  • Primary pathology (acute MI, post-cardiotomy, myocarditis, etc.)
  • Evidence of myocardial recovery
  • LV distension and risk of stasis/thrombus
  • RV function
  • Duration of support
  • Complications encountered

‚öñÔ∏è Complex Balancing Act

Weaning ECMO and Impella requires balancing:

  • LV unloading (Impella benefit) vs systemic perfusion (ECMO benefit)
  • Myocardial work (increased as support reduced) vs recovery time (benefit of continued rest)
  • Device-related complications (thrombosis, bleeding, infection) vs continued support benefit

Individual Assessment Required

This is a complex balance, with individual assessment required at multiple time points.

Serial assessments should include:

  • Daily multidisciplinary rounds with imaging review
  • Regular echocardiography (at least daily, more frequently during active weaning)
  • Trial reductions in support with close monitoring
  • Biomarker trends (troponin, BNP)
  • Assessment of end-organ function

Impella Removal During VA-ECMO

üè• Surgical Removal Required

The Impella should be removed in cardiac theatres with primary vascular repair of the vessel for all patients on ECMO.

üíâ Anticoagulation Management

Removal should be performed with heparin anticoagulation still running

This differs from standard Impella removal protocols and is necessary to:

  • Maintain ECMO circuit patency
  • Reduce risk of thrombotic complications
  • Allow surgical haemostasis under controlled conditions

Surgical Removal Procedure

Location: Cardiac theatres

Team required:

  • Cardiac surgeon or vascular surgeon
  • Anaesthetist
  • Cardiac theatre nursing team
  • ECMO specialist (to manage ECMO during procedure)
  • Interventional cardiologist (advisory role)

Procedure:

  • Maintain full ECMO support throughout
  • Continue heparin infusion
  • Surgical exposure of femoral artery
  • Controlled removal of Impella catheter
  • Primary vascular repair with sutures
  • Assessment of distal limb perfusion
  • Wound closure

Post-Removal Care

Following Impella removal in a patient still on VA-ECMO:

  • Continue ECMO support as per plan
  • Monitor limb perfusion closely
  • Watch for bleeding from surgical site
  • Serial assessment of haemodynamics
  • Consider need for ongoing LV unloading strategies if indicated
