Section 7: Removal Procedures

Version 3.2 | March 2025 – December 2026

⚠️ Important: The decision to remove the device and the process to follow should be guided by the interventional cardiology consultant. A senior medic from either the cardiology or ICU team should be primarily in charge of removing the device and should not leave the patient's bedside until haemostasis has been confirmed.

Initial Step (All Removals)

  1. When the decision to remove the device is made, reduce the P-level to P1 and pull the Impella catheter back across the aortic valve.

In the Cath Lab (with introducer sheath still in situ)

Cath Lab Removal Procedure:

  • With the 14F introducer sheath in place switch the Impella off and remove the catheter through the introducer
  • Stop the heparin, and monitor the ACT. Once below 150s remove the 14F sheath
  • Groin management depends on use of closure devices and hospital protocol

In ICU (with the repositioning sheath)

ICU Removal Procedure:

  1. Stop the heparin and perform a TEG at 2 hours
  2. Wait until the TEG-ACT is <150s – continue to repeat the TEG 2 hourly until this is the case
  3. At this stage switch Impella off and remove both the catheter and repositioning sheath together
  4. Apply manual pressure initially for 15 minutes, followed by a femstop

If there are any concerns regarding haemostasis, the vascular team on call (based at NBT) should be contacted for assistance in removal.

Weaning During VA-ECMO

ECMO, interventional and imaging cardiology consultants together will make the decision about weaning of both the ECMO and Impella device flows. This is related to assessment of native contractility/output, LV size, reduction in pulmonary congestion and overall haemodynamics. It is a complex balance, with individual assessment required at multiple time points.

The Impella should be removed in theatres with primary vascular repair of the vessel for all patients on ECMO – and with the heparin anti-coagulation still running.
